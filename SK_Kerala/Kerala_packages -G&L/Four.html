Itinerary:

  • DAY 1: Kochi (Cochin)

    • Kochi, also known as Cochin, is a beautiful city in Kerala, often called the "Queen of the Arabian Sea." It is famous for its mix of old and new, with historic places like the Chinese fishing nets, Mattancherry Palace, and the Jewish Synagogue. Visitors can enjoy peaceful backwaters, green landscapes, and beaches like Fort Kochi Beach. The city is also known for its art and culture, with events like the Biennale and traditional Kathakali shows. Kochi’s spice markets and tasty seafood make it a favorite spot for travelers.

  • DAY 2: Munnar

    • Munnar, located in the Western Ghats of Kerala, is a stunning hill station known for its breathtaking beauty and pleasant climate. Famous for its sprawling tea plantations, rolling hills, and misty landscapes, it is a paradise for nature lovers and peace seekers. Visitors can explore attractions like Eravikulam National Park, home to the endangered Nilgiri Tahr, and Anamudi Peak, the highest point in South India. The serene Mattupetty Dam, Echo Point, and lush forests add to Munnar’s charm. With its fresh mountain air and picturesque views, Munnar is the perfect getaway for a relaxing and memorable holiday.

  • DAY 3: Thekkady

    • Thekkady, in Kerala, is a beautiful place known for its green forests and wildlife. It is home to the Periyar Wildlife Sanctuary, where you can take boat rides on the lake and see animals like elephants, deer, and many birds. Thekkady is also famous for its spice plantations, where you can learn about cardamom, pepper, and cinnamon. Visitors can enjoy activities like trekking, bamboo rafting, and nature walks. With its cool weather and peaceful surroundings, Thekkady is a great spot for nature and adventure lovers.

  • DAY 4: Alleppey

    • Alleppey, also known as Alappuzha, is a charming town in Kerala, famous for its scenic backwaters and houseboat cruises. Often called the "Venice of the East," it offers a tranquil escape with its network of canals, lagoons, and lakes surrounded by lush greenery. Visitors can enjoy a unique stay in traditional houseboats, savoring delicious Kerala cuisine while floating on calm waters. The town is also known for its serene beaches, like Alappuzha Beach, and the annual Nehru Trophy Boat Race, a thrilling spectacle. Alleppey is a perfect destination for relaxation, natural beauty, and a glimpse of Kerala's unique culture.
