Itinerary:

  • DAY 1: Puri

    • Puri is a coastal city in the Indian state of Odisha, known for its religious significance and beautiful beaches. It is famous for the Jagannath Temple, a major pilgrimage site for Hindus. Every year, the city hosts the famous Rath Yatra (chariot festival), attracting millions of devotees. Puri is also a popular tourist destination for its golden beaches and vibrant local culture. The city has a rich history and is an important part of the cultural heritage of Odisha.

  • DAY 2: Satapada

    • Satapada is a small town located near Chilika Lake, in the Puri district of Odisha, India. It is known for its natural beauty and is a popular tourist destination. Satapada is famous for its Dolphin Sanctuary, where visitors can spot the rare Irrawaddy dolphins in the waters of Chilika Lake. The area is also known for its serene landscapes, birdwatching opportunities, and boat rides on the lake. The lake is a haven for migratory birds, especially during the winter season. Satapada is a peaceful getaway, combining wildlife, nature, and local culture.

  • DAY 3: Konark

    • Konark is a small town in Odisha, India, famous for the stunning Konark Sun Temple. This 13th-century temple, a UNESCO World Heritage Site, is shaped like a massive chariot with intricately carved wheels, showcasing exquisite architecture. The temple is dedicated to the Sun God and is a major pilgrimage and tourist destination. Konark is also known for its annual dance festival, which celebrates classical Indian dance forms. The town has a rich history and cultural significance, making it an important part of Odishaâ€™s heritage.

  • Day 4: Kolkata

    • After breakfast, check out from the hotel and proceed to kolkata Airport/Railway Station for your onward journey.

  • DAY 5: Dakhineswar and Belurmath

    • Dakshineswar is a town located near Kolkata, West Bengal, known for the famous Dakshineswar Kali Temple. The temple is dedicated to Goddess Kali and was built in the 19th century by Rani Rashmoni. It is a significant pilgrimage site and attracts thousands of devotees every year. Dakshineswar is also famous for its connection to the spiritual teacher Sri Ramakrishna Paramahamsa, who spent much of his life here. The town is situated on the banks of the Hooghly River, offering beautiful views and a peaceful atmosphere.
    Belur Math is a renowned spiritual center located in Howrah, West Bengal, on the banks of the Hooghly River. It is the headquarters of the Ramakrishna Math and Mission, founded by Swami Vivekananda. The Math is dedicated to the teachings of Sri Ramakrishna, his disciples, and the values of universal brotherhood and religious harmony. The main temple at Belur Math is known for its beautiful architecture, combining Hindu, Christian, and Islamic styles. It is a popular pilgrimage site, attracting visitors from all over the world for meditation, spiritual learning, and peace.

  • DAY 6: Howrah

    • It seems like you might be referring to "Howrah," a city in the Indian state of West Bengal. Howrah is an important industrial and transportation hub, located near Kolkata. It is famous for the Howrah Bridge, a major landmark that connects Kolkata to Howrah over the Hooghly River. The city is a key part of the Kolkata Metropolitan Area and is known for its bustling markets, vibrant culture, and historical sites. Howrah also houses many industries, including steel, engineering, and textiles, making it an economic center.
